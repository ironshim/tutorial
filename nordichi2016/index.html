Tutorial: Subjective and Objective Replication and Replicability

Introduction

This page contains additional content for the Tutorial on Subjective and Objective Replication and Replicability at the 2016 Nordic Conference on Human-Computer Interaction.

Instructors

  • Alan Said
  • Gustaf Lindblad

Tutorial Outline

09:00 - 09:30 Introduction

Introduction and walk-through of what we will be presenting and why it is important. Common pitfalls and caveats etc.

09:30 - 10:00 Interpretive data collection

Annotation, coding, and other aspects important in data collection.

10:00 - 10:30 User data, metrics, methods, and algorithms

Impact of design choices in data science experiments. How the same data, same algorithm and metrics can have widely different results. What needs to done to minimize the effects of this. MLOSS

10:30 - 11:00 Coffee break

11:00 - 12:00 Hands on work and examples

Data collection
Use of data

12:00 - 12:30 Conclusions and Q&A

How does open versus proprietary software affect the various things discussed and presented in the tutorial.

Learning Outcome

The attendees are expected to gain understanding into how to present research in a clear and concise fashion. These skills will aid the attendees to report the settings, experiments, studies, and results of their research in a manner that better supports reproduction and replication.
